1:
ar: اَللَّهُمَّ رَبَّ السَّمَوَاتِ السَّبْعِ وَمَا أَظَلَّتْ، وَرَبَّ الْأَرَضَيْنِ وَمَا أَقَلَّتْ، وَرَبَّ الشَّيَاطِيْنِ وَمَا أَضَلَّتْ، كُنْ لِيْ جَارًا مِنْ شَرِّ خَلْقِكَ أَجْمَعِيْنَ، أَنْ يَّفْرُطَ عَلَيَّ أَحَدٌ مِنْهُمْ وَأَنْ يَّطْغٰى، عَزَّ جَارُكَ، وَتَبَارَكَ اسْمُكَ

"O Allah, Lord of the seven heavens and of that on which they cast their shadows, and Lord of the earths and of that which they hold, and Lord of the devils and of that which they mislead, be my protection against the evil of all of your creation lest they should trespass on me or oppress me. Strong is your protection and blessed is your Name."
ref: (Tabarani Al Kabeer(Urdu) 3747, Tabarani in Awsat, Ibn Abu Shaybah narrated by Khalid Ibn Waleed)
---
2:
ar: اَللَّهُمَّ غَارَتِ النُّجُوْمُ، وهَدَأَتِ الْعُيُوْنُ، وَأَنْتَ حَيٌّ قَيُّوْمٌ لَا تَأْخُذُكَ سِنَةٌ وَلَا نَوْمٌ، يَا حَيُّ يَا قَيُّومُ، أَهْدِئْ لَيْلِيْ، وَأَنِمْ عَيْنِيْ

"O Allah! The stars have sunk and the eyes have gone to rest while you are the Living, The Eternal, Sleep does not overtake you, nor slumber, O the Living, O The Eternal! Let my night be peaceful and let my eyes sleep!"
ref: (Ibn Hibban 2/291, Ibn Sunni, narrated by Zayd Ibn Thabit).
